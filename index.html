Lata Mangeshkar

1929-2022

Nightingale of India

Lata Mangeshkar, born on September 28, 1929, in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, India, and passing away on February 6, 2022, in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India, was one of India's most iconic and beloved playback singers. Her illustrious career began in 1942 when she was just 13 years old, and over the decades, she lent her voice to thousands of songs across more than thirty-six languages, earning the affectionate title "Nightingale of India." Lata's voice became emblematic of Bollywood's golden era, and her collaborations with eminent music directors and lyricists resulted in timeless classics such as "Aye Mere Watan Ke Logon," "Lag Jaa Gale," "Pyar Kiya To Darna Kya," and "Tere Bina Zindagi Se."

Her contributions to music were recognized with numerous awards, including theBharat Ratna in 2001, Padma Bhushan in 1969, Padma Vibhushan in 1999, and theDadasaheb Phalke Award in 1989. She also won multiple National Film Awards andFilmfare Awards for Best Female Playback Singer. Beyond her musical achievements,Lata Mangeshkar was known for her philanthropy, particularly through the LataMangeshkar Medical Foundation, which provides healthcare services to those inneed. Despite her immense fame, she remained devoted to her music and family,never marrying and continuing to inspire generations with her soulful voice andemotional depth in her songs. Lata Mangeshkar's legacy as a musical legendendures, leaving an indelible mark on the Indian music industry.
